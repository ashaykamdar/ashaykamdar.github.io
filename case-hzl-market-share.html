Regaining Market Share at Hindustan Zinc (HZL)

Context & Problem

Hindustan Zinc’s market share had fallen to approximately 65%. The root cause was not product quality or pricing, but a structural limitation in the sales model. The company relied almost entirely on direct OEM sales, resulting in limited geographic reach and an inability to tap into trader-led demand. There was no formal or governed channel partner ecosystem in place.

Objective

Reverse the decline and increase market share by 5% within 6 months, without aggressive pricing or margin dilution.

My Role

As a Business Analyst in the CMO’s Office, I worked on strategy formulation, channel design, partner evaluation, and execution tracking.

Solution & Strategy

I designed and helped launch a Pan-India Channel Partner (CP) Program to complement OEM sales. The strategy transitioned HZL from a direct-only model to a hybrid OEM + CP sales model, enabling rapid expansion into underserved regions.

Channel Partner Selection Framework

To reduce execution risk, I built a quantitative, weighted CP selection framework. Each partner was evaluated on:

  • Last 3 years turnover and Profit After Tax
  • Number of warehouses and trading infrastructure
  • Annual trading volume
  • Pan-India reach
  • Audited sales day books and compliance standards

Post-Selection Analytics

Post onboarding, I analyzed CP sales day books to validate policy effectiveness and identify risks:

  • Region-wise performance analysis
  • B2B vs B2C sales mix
  • New vs repeat customer behavior
  • Segment concentration and leakage risks

Impact

  • +5% market share growth within 6 months
  • ~₹1,000 Cr incremental business
  • Built a scalable, governed channel ecosystem
