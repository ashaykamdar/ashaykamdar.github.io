Building an AI Mock Interview Platform (0→1) at UPivot

Context

UPivot was an early-stage startup focused on helping PM aspirants crack interviews. I joined as a founding team member and Associate Product Manager, working on the product from zero to launch.

User Problem

Through early research, we found that PM aspirants:

  • Did not know how interviews actually went
  • Lacked clarity on qualitative vs quantitative gaps
  • Received generic, non-JD-aligned mock interviews
  • Struggled with confidence and communication

Objective

Build a realistic, feedback-driven mock interview platform that mirrors real PM interviews and helps users improve meaningfully.

Discovery & Research

I conducted extensive user interviews, surveys, and secondary research. Structured questionnaires helped identify where users dropped off and what feedback they valued most.

Solution

We built an AI-powered mock interview platform where users could:

  • Select interview topic, interviewer type, and difficulty
  • Upload resume and job description
  • Receive structured, actionable feedback

Execution & Iteration

I drove low-fidelity prototyping, A/B testing, and rapid iteration based on usage data. Initial data showed that only 46 out of 100 users started interviews due to friction. After simplifying the flow, engagement improved by 28%.

Mid-interview drop-offs (~50%) were addressed by reducing cognitive load, cutting unnecessary steps, and improving instructions, leading to a 25% reduction in drop-offs.

Impact

  • 1,200+ users onboarded on Day 1
  • +28% engagement after friction reduction
  • 25% drop-off reduction mid-interview
