Automating MCX–LME Arbitrage at Hindustan Zinc

Context & Problem

Hindustan Zinc actively participates in commodity markets where profitability is highly sensitive to arbitrage opportunities between MCX (India) and LME (global). However, the arbitrage tracking process was fully manual.

Teams tracked MCX and LME prices separately, calculated spreads manually, and relied on human judgment to decide execution. This resulted in:

  • Missing ~4 out of every 10 arbitrage opportunities
  • Delayed reaction times in volatile markets
  • High dependency on experienced manpower costing ~₹12 LPA
  • Each missed opportunity causing losses ranging from lakhs to crores

Objective

Improve arbitrage capture, reduce reaction time, and eliminate manual dependency without increasing operational cost.

My Role

Business Analyst — problem structuring, requirement definition, logic design, and impact tracking.

Solution

I worked on designing an automated MCX–LME arbitrage tracking system that continuously monitored price differentials in real time.

The system:

  • Fetched live MCX and LME prices
  • Calculated real-time arbitrage spreads
  • Triggered threshold-based alerts when profitable windows opened
  • Removed the need for manual tracking and calculations

Execution & Decision Enablement

By standardizing thresholds and automating alerts, the system enabled faster, more objective decisions. Traders could act immediately rather than wait for manual confirmation, significantly reducing latency in high-volatility windows.

Impact

  • 23% increase in overall profitability
  • Prevented high-value opportunity losses
  • Eliminated ~₹12 LPA manpower cost
  • Enabled faster, data-driven arbitrage execution
